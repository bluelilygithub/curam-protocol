üéØ Accuracy & Risk Management

Questions about AI accuracy, error prevention, and risk mitigation.

We use a three-layer validation system, targeting 99%+ production accuracy:

  • Grounding: The AI only extracts data from your documents‚Äîit is incapable of generating or guessing information from the internet.
  • Confidence Scoring: If the AI scores <90% confident on any item, it flags the record for human review by YOUR staff through a simple approval queue.
  • ERP Cross-Checks: We validate extracted data against your existing vendor master lists, project codes, and approval thresholds for hard-coded correctness.

When rare errors occur (usually edge cases like damaged scans), they route to your team within seconds for quick correction, maintaining high automation efficiency.

The concern about failure concentrating on critical documents is precisely why Phase 1 (Feasibility Sprint) is mandatory: we eliminate this risk upfront.

We first work with your team to identify and verify the perfect, correct answer for the most important data points on your structural plans‚Äîwe call this the 'answer key' or Ground Truth. Our ‚â•90% pass/fail guarantee is then measured only against that critical, verified answer key.

This means a successful test on simple documents cannot mask a failure on a critical document; if the AI cannot reliably handle the most complex, high-stakes information you submitted, we issue a full refund and stop the project immediately.

Our validation layers are designed to flag errors before they reach your ERP. If an error does bypass the system (e.g., a critical field is misread):

  • Workflow: The human review process ensures the data is corrected and re-entered quickly.
  • Feedback Loop: The incorrect document/data immediately enters a feedback loop to retrain the AI model for that specific edge case.
  • Professional Indemnity: We carry Professional Indemnity insurance which covers errors made by the AI implementation. Details of our policy are provided during the contract phase.

Our Phase 1 Time Saved figures are deliberately conservative because they already account for the minimal human intervention required for the 'Fail Safe' review process. This is not cleanup; it is a high-value risk mitigation step that affects <10% of documents, ensuring the overall efficiency gain and the ROI remain verifiably intact.
