About Curam-Ai Protocol

Your Path to Responsible AI Adoption

Our Mission

At Curam-Ai Protocol, we understand the unique challenges faced by mid-sized engineering firms when considering AI adoption. Stability, predictability, and control are not just priorities—they are necessities. Our mission is to provide a disciplined, fixed-scope approach that de-risks AI adoption and ensures predictable value.

The Curam-Ai Protocol Framework

Our framework is designed specifically for established SMEs that need to control their growth without sacrificing the rigor and certainty that have defined their business for decades. Here's how our structured, four-phase Protocol works:

Phase 1: The Feasibility Sprint

Objective: Confirm technical feasibility with minimal initial investment.

Process:

  • Conduct a direct, paid feasibility test to swiftly prove the technical capacity to extract data from your specific engineering documents.
  • Deliver a "Validated Benchmark" confirming a high accuracy standard.
  • If necessary accuracy cannot be achieved, trigger an immediate "Off-Ramp" with a full assessment and findings memo.

Outcome: Ensure your firm only moves forward based on objective proof of concept.

Phase 2: The Readiness Roadmap

Objective: Establish strategic readiness and financial justification.

Process:

  • Move beyond technical feasibility to deliver a "Board-Ready Business Case."
  • Gather volume metrics and analyze billable rates to quantify the precise Return on Investment (ROI) for the entire project.
  • Provide a clear risk register and long-term support plan.

Outcome: Secure buy-in from Partners and Leadership with concrete financial data.

Phase 3: The Compliance Shield

Objective: Ensure data security and compliance before deployment.

Process:

  • Conduct a proactive, pre-audit check to address CTO concerns regarding data sovereignty, security, and liability.
  • Map the deployment against standards like ISO 27001 and validate all cloud tenancy requirements.
  • Build a comprehensive Compliance Pool of documentation.

Outcome: Flag potential technical or compliance blockers early, ensuring rigorous compliance.

Phase 4: Wave 1 Implementation

Objective: Achieve a fixed-scope, short-duration sprint to production.

Process:

  • Focus on direct integration into existing workflows using low-code or process automation tools.
  • Ensure Full IP Handover, providing your firm with the source code and workflows outright.

Outcome: Eliminate vendor lock-in, avoid ongoing licensing fees, and guarantee self-sufficiency.
